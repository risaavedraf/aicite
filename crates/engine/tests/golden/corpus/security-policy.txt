Information Security and Compliance Policy
============================================

Effective Date: 2026-01-01
Document Owner: Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)
Classification: Internal — Controlled Distribution
Review Cycle: Annual or upon material change

1. Purpose
----------
This policy establishes the minimum security controls, data-handling requirements, and compliance obligations for all personnel, contractors, and third-party systems that access organizational resources. Its intent is to protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information assets while meeting applicable regulatory obligations.

2. Scope
--------
This policy applies to all employees, contractors, consultants, temporary workers, and any third party granted access to organizational systems, networks, data, or physical facilities. It covers all environments including production, staging, development, and disaster-recovery sites.

3. Authentication and Password Policy
--------------------------------------
All user accounts must enforce strong authentication credentials. Passwords must be minimum 12 characters with at least one uppercase, one lowercase, one digit, and one special character. Passwords must not reuse any of the user's previous 12 passwords. Multi-factor authentication (MFA) is mandatory for all remote access, administrative interfaces, and any system storing classified or regulated data. Accounts that remain inactive for 60 consecutive days are automatically disabled.

4. Data Encryption Standards
-----------------------------
All data at rest is encrypted using AES-256, and data in transit uses TLS 1.3 minimum. Encryption keys are managed through a centralized key-management service with automated rotation every 365 days or immediately upon suspected compromise. Backup media must also be encrypted using the same standards before off-site transport. Deprecated protocols such as SSL, TLS 1.0, TLS 1.1, and TLS 1.2 are prohibited for any new deployment.

5. Audit Logging and Monitoring
--------------------------------
All access events are logged with timestamp, user ID, resource accessed, and action performed, retained for 90 days. Logs must be immutable, stored in a write-once medium, and accessible only to authorized security personnel. Anomalous patterns — including repeated failed authentication attempts, privilege escalation, and bulk data export — trigger automated alerts to the Security Operations Center (SOC) within five minutes. Log integrity is verified quarterly through hash-chain validation.

6. Data Classification and Handling
-------------------------------------
Data is classified into four tiers: Public, Internal, Confidential, and Restricted. Each tier prescribes minimum controls for storage, transmission, and disposal. Restricted data requires executive approval for access and must never leave approved environments without Data Loss Prevention (DLP) scanning.

7. Incident Response
---------------------
Security incidents must be reported within one hour of detection. The incident response team classifies severity, contains the threat, and initiates forensic preservation. Post-incident reviews are completed within five business days and findings are incorporated into this policy as needed.

8. Third-Party Risk Management
-------------------------------
Vendors with access to organizational data must complete a security assessment annually. Contracts must include data-protection clauses, breach-notification obligations aligned with applicable privacy regulations, and the right to audit.

9. Acceptable Use
------------------
Organizational systems are for business purposes. Users must not install unauthorized software, bypass security controls, or share credentials. Personal devices connecting to the corporate network must comply with the Mobile Device Management (MDM) baseline.

10. Policy Enforcement
-----------------------
Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination and legal referral. All personnel must acknowledge understanding of this policy upon onboarding and annually thereafter.
